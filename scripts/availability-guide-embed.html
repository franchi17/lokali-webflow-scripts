Your availability calendar:
take bookings without the back‑and‑forth
Stop hand-drawing a sold-out calendar on Instagram. Show neighbors which dates are open, let them request a date (or a time), and confirm each one yourself — nothing is booked until you say yes. Included with Pro and Featured plans.
The big idea
You stay in control — confirm to reserve
Customers see a simple calendar on your storefront with each date marked OpenLimitedSold out. They pick a date, tell you what they want, and send a request. It lands in your Availability inbox as a lead — capacity only moves when you tap Confirm. Confirming reserves their spot and emails them automatically. Declining frees you up, no awkward conversation needed.
Your numbers stay private. Customers never see how many orders you have — only whether a date is open, running low, or sold out. The exact counts show only to you, in your dashboard.
First choice
Pick the mode that matches how you sell
Open Dashboard → Availability → Settings and choose one of two modes. You can switch later, but most vendors are clearly one or the other.
By quantity
You make a limited number per day
Bakers, meal preppers, jam makers, caterers. You set how many orders you can take per day (say, 30), customers pick a date and how many they want. Each confirm counts down that day's total.
By time slot
You book appointments
Photographers, hair stylists, tutors, massage therapists. You set your weekly hours and a booking length — Lokali generates the time slots automatically, and customers pick a date and a time.
Your schedule
Hours: set them once, they do double duty
The Hours section is one weekly schedule — for each day, add the window you're open (like 9:00 to 5:00). It repeats every week and shows on your storefront as your hours of operation. In time-slot mode it does more: your bookable appointments are generated inside those windows.
1
Closing for lunch? Add two windows
Add 9:00–12:00 and 1:00–5:00 on the same day. The gap between them is your break — customers see the split hours, and no appointments are offered over lunch.
2
Booking length & buffer shape your slots
Set how long one booking runs (60 minutes, 30 minutes…) and an optional buffer between bookings (10 minutes to reset, travel, clean up). A 12–4 window with 60-minute bookings and a 10-minute buffer becomes 12:00, 1:10, 2:20 and 3:30 — done for you.
3
One day can march to its own beat
Tap any window's timing pill to give just that window a custom length or buffer — say, quick 30-minute consults on Saturday mornings while the rest of the week runs hour-long sessions.
4
Check the preview before you move on
Under each window you'll see exactly which times customers will be offered. If a slot's missing, the window is too short for the booking length — widen the window or shorten the length.
Settings, in plain english
Four dials, and what they really mean
Everything else in Settings is one of these. Set them once — you can always nudge them later.
Orders per day
Your daily cap
quantity mode — how many you can make in one day
Show “Limited” at
The urgency line
when this few remain, the date shows “Limited” — a nudge to order soon
Minimum notice
Your heads-up time
how far ahead people must request — 12 hours means no midnight orders for tomorrow morning
When a customer requests
Hold, or don’t
“Hold on inquiry” saves their spot while you decide (auto-released if you don’t answer); “Hold on confirm” reserves nothing until you say yes
Not sure about holds? Start with “Hold on confirm.” It's the simplest: requests are just leads, and nothing is promised until you tap Confirm. Switch to “Hold on inquiry” if you find two people racing for the same spot.
Day to day
Your daily rhythm on the Availability page
Once it's set up, the page runs on three simple habits.
1
Check Requests — confirm or decline
New date-tagged requests wait at the top, grouped by date with how much of that day is left. Confirm reserves the spot and emails the customer automatically. Decline frees it. Lokali guards against overselling — if a confirm would put you past your cap, it stops you.
2
Tap dates to block days off
Vacation, market day, family weekend — tap any date on the Days off & capacity calendar to block it. Customers see it as unavailable instantly. Tap again to reopen it. The little numbers on each date (like 12/30 orders or 2/4 slots booked) are your private at-a-glance workload.
3
Let sold-out days sell for you (Featured)
On the Featured plan, a sold-out date shows a waitlist — customers leave their email, and if a spot frees up you tap “Offer spot” to invite the next person in line. Every cancellation becomes a warm lead instead of a lost sale.
Ready to turn it on? Go to Dashboard → Availability, pick your mode, set your hours, and flip “Calendar on.” Your storefront gets an Availability tab the moment you save — and every request it brings lands right back in that dashboard for you to confirm.
